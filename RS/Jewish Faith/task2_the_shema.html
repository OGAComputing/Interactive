📜 The Shema

The Anchor of Faith

The Shema is the most important prayer in Judaism. It is remarkable because it fits into ALL THREE marks of faith — belief, action, and symbol. Read the prayer carefully, then work through the activities.

📌 Key Facts

The Shema is recited twice daily — morning and evening. It is often the first prayer a child learns and the last words said before death. When reciting the first line, many Jewish people cover their eyes to concentrate fully. The words appear inside the Tefillin (worn on the body) and the Mezuzah (on the doorpost).

שְׁמַע יִשְׂרָאֵל — The Shema
"Hear, Israel, the Lord is our G-d, the Lord is One."
Blessed be the Name of His glorious kingdom forever and ever.
And you shall love the Lord your G-d with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your might.
And these words that I command you today shall be in your heart.
And you shall teach them diligently to your children, and you shall speak of them when you sit at home, and when you walk along the way, and when you lie down and when you rise up.
And you shall bind them as a sign on your hand, and they shall be for frontlets between your eyes.
And you shall write them on the doorposts of your house and on your gates.

✅ Activity A — True or False?

Check Your Understanding

Decide whether each statement about the Shema is true or false. Click your answer — feedback will appear immediately.

1. The Shema begins with the words "Hear, O Israel: the Lord our G-d, the Lord is One."
✅ True. This opening declaration of monotheism — that G-d is One — is the heart of the prayer and one of the most important statements in all of Judaism.
2. The Shema is only recited once a week, at the synagogue on Shabbat.
❌ False. The Shema is recited twice daily — morning and evening — and can be said anywhere, not just at the synagogue. The prayer says "when you lie down and when you rise up."
3. The words of the Shema are written on a scroll inside the Mezuzah, which is placed on the doorpost of a Jewish home.
✅ True. The Shema directly commands "write them on the doorposts of your house." The Mezuzah fulfils this commandment — making it a Symbol as well as a Belief.
4. The Shema teaches that Jewish children do not need to learn the prayer — it is only for adults.
❌ False. The Shema specifically says to "teach them diligently to your children." It is often one of the first prayers a Jewish child learns, and is passed down through generations.
5. Tefillin are worn on the arm and forehead during prayer because the Shema says to "bind them as a sign on your hand."
✅ True. This is a direct fulfilment of the Shema's commandment. The Tefillin contain the Shema on a scroll — so wearing them is simultaneously a Belief (the words), an Action (wearing/praying), and a Symbol (visible on the body).
6. The Shema is a symbol only — it does not count as a belief or an action.
❌ False. The Shema is unique precisely because it fits ALL THREE marks of faith: it expresses a Belief (G-d is One), requires an Action (recited twice daily), and is connected to Symbols (Mezuzah, Tefillin).
